what are asteroids

Asteroids are small, rocky objects that orbit the Sun, like planets but much smaller in size. They are remnants left over from the early formation of our solar system about 4.6 billion years ago. In popular culture, asteroids are sometimes confused with meteoroids, meteors, and meteorites. When an asteroid enters Earth's atmosphere and burns up, it's called a meteor. If a fragment of that meteor reaches the Earth's surface, it's called a meteorite. Overall, asteroids are intriguing celestial bodies that offer clues about the early solar system and the formation of planets. They're also important to monitor for planetary defence reasons.
